## üìñ Task Explanations

Today, you'll learn one of the most important concepts in mobile app development: navigation. Navigation is how users move between different screens in your app. In Flutter, navigation is managed by the `Navigator` widget, which maintains a stack of routes (screens).

Think of the navigation stack like a stack of plates. When you navigate to a new screen, you "push" it onto the stack. When you go back, you "pop" the top screen off the stack. This is why `Navigator.push()` takes you forward and `Navigator.pop()` takes you back.

You'll start with the simplest form of navigation: pushing a new screen onto the stack. This is the foundation for all navigation in Flutter, and understanding it is crucial before moving on to more advanced navigation patterns.

## üìö Dictionary for Developers

*   **Navigation:** The process of moving between different screens or views in an application.
*   **`Navigator`:** A Flutter widget that manages a stack of routes (screens) and provides methods to navigate between them.
*   **Route:** A representation of a screen or page in Flutter's navigation system.
*   **Navigation Stack:** A stack data structure that holds the history of screens the user has navigated to, allowing them to go back.
*   **`Navigator.push()`:** A method that adds a new route to the navigation stack, navigating to a new screen.
*   **`Navigator.pop()`:** A method that removes the current route from the navigation stack, returning to the previous screen.
