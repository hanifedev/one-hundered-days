## ğŸ“– Task Explanations

Today, you'll learn how to interact with widgets in your tests, simulating user actions like tapping, typing, and swiping. This is crucial for testing that your widgets respond correctly to user input.

The `WidgetTester` provides various methods for interaction:
- **`tap()`:** Simulates a tap on a widget
- **`enterText()`:** Types text into a `TextField`
- **`drag()`:** Simulates dragging a widget
- **`fling()`:** Simulates a fling/swipe gesture

After interactions, you often need to wait for the UI to update. Flutter uses `pump()` to advance time and trigger rebuilds. `pumpAndSettle()` waits for all animations and async operations to complete, which is useful after interactions that trigger animations or async work.

You'll practice by testing a form widget, which involves entering text, tapping buttons, and verifying that the form behaves correctly. This gives you practical experience with common testing scenarios.
