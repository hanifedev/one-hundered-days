## ğŸ“– Task Explanations

A good testing strategy helps you write the right tests at the right level. Not everything needs to be tested, and over-testing can be as problematic as under-testing. Understanding what to test (user-visible behavior) and what not to test (implementation details) helps you write focused, valuable tests.

Test organization and maintenance are crucial as applications grow. Well-organized tests are easier to find, understand, and maintain. Following best practices ensures your tests remain valuable and don't become a burden as your codebase evolves.
