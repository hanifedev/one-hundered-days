## ğŸ“– Task Explanations

The Saga pattern is essential for managing distributed transactions in microservices architectures. Unlike traditional ACID transactions, Sagas use a series of local transactions with compensation logic. This enables distributed systems to maintain data consistency across services.

Understanding the Saga pattern enables you to build microservices that can coordinate complex operations reliably. It's a fundamental pattern for distributed systems.

## ğŸ“š Dictionary for Developers

*   **Saga Pattern:** A pattern for managing distributed transactions using a series of local transactions with compensation.
*   **Distributed Transaction:** A transaction that spans multiple services or databases.
*   **Compensation:** A rollback mechanism that undoes the effects of previous operations in a Saga.
*   **Orchestration:** A Saga approach where a central coordinator manages the transaction flow.
*   **Choreography:** A Saga approach where each service knows what to do next without a central coordinator.
