Stack Navigator is the most common navigation pattern in mobile apps. It works like a stack of cardsâ€”when you navigate to a new screen, it's pushed onto the stack. ## ğŸ“š Dictionary for Developers

*   **Stack Navigator:** A navigator that manages screens in a stack structure, where new screens are pushed on top and can be popped off when going back.
*   **`navigation.navigate()`:** A method to navigate to a screen (can reuse existing screens in the stack).
*   **`navigation.push()`:** A method that always pushes a new screen onto the stack.
*   **`navigation.goBack()`:** A method that pops the current screen from the stack and returns to the previous one.
*   **Navigation Header:** The top bar of a screen that typically shows the title and navigation controls.
